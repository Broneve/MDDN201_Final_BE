Intro

Bellow are some of the best Renaissance artist that have ever been. The Renaisance movement came after the Medieval movement and has stong ties to christianity and the church. Artworks were done mainly with oil on canvas and has an emphasis upon the close observation of nature in regards to human anatomy. Renaisance artists also focused on scientific principles in regards to the use of perspective and light.

Gentile Bellini

Gentile Bellini was born in Venice Italy in 1429. His father Jacopo Bellini and brother Giovanni Bellini were also painters in Venice. Gentile was trained by his father in which you can see his influence in Gentile’s earlier works. He became an independent artist and eventually was sent to Constantinople by the Doge of Venice.

Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was a Renaissance painter, sculptor, architect, inventor, and military engineer. Da Vinci studied the laws of science and nature, which influenced his works. Da Vinci was born just outside the village of Anchiano in Tuscany, Italy on April 15, 1452. Born out of wedlock to respected Florentine notary Ser Piero and a young peasant woman named Caterina, da Vinci was raised by his father and his stepmother. Young da Vinci received little formal education beyond basic reading, writing and mathematics. At around the age of 14, Da Vinci began an apprenticeship with the artist Andrea del Verrocchio in Florence. He learned a wide variety of technical skills including metalworking, leather arts, carpentry, drawing, painting and sculpting.

Donatello

Donatello was born Donato di Niccolo di Betto Bardi in Florence, Italy, in 1386. The name “Donatello” came from his friends and family as a nickname. His father, Niccolo di Betto Bardi, was a member of the Florentine Wool Combers Guild. This gave Donatello the status of a son of a craftsman and placed him on a path of working in the trades. Donatello was educated at the home of the Martelli's, a wealthy and influential Florentine family of bankers and art patrons closely tied to the Medici family.
